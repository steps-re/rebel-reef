The Rebel Reef, the documentary

"A film about resilience." The Rebel Reef follows Christian Carias, Tela's sole divemaster, as he helps discover and protect a coral reef that defies scientific expectations, while recovering from a near-fatal motorcycle accident. It is a story about healing and hope in the face of ecological loss, set in the hot, silty, unlikely waters where this reef persists against the odds.

Exec. producer: Tiffany Duong (Tela Coral) Lead scientist: Dr. Juli Berwald With: Antal Börcsök · Tela Marine

The film is premiering at select festivals and is not yet available for public viewing. Community and private screenings can be requested.

Tela Coral, the foundation

Tela Coral is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit working to understand and protect the reefs of Tela Bay, Honduras, which stay astonishingly healthy while reefs collapse worldwide. Their mission is to uncover Tela's extraordinary health and preserve what they call the Rebel Reef.

Their work spans scientific expeditions (coral genetics, skeleton-core analysis, cryptic coral lineages), the documentary, education programs, and the Music for the Ocean tour, which pairs live performance with ocean-conservation storytelling.

Home to a dozen critically endangered coral species, including elkhorn.
Astonishingly low disease and bleaching versus the wider Caribbean.
Music for the Ocean reached 500+ attendees across Japan.
Leads the research and protection of Banco Capiro and Cocalito.
