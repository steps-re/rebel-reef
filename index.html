Tela Coral · Rebel Reef Banco Capiro / Cocalito · 15.90°N 87.48°W

The reef that shouldn't exist.

Banco Capiro holds 60–70% live coral in warm, murky, sewage-fed water that is killing the famous clear reefs 30–60 km away. It wasn't spared the heat — it took a lethal dose and lived. This page maps why, from first principles.

The mystery in one line

Nature ran a controlled experiment: same ocean, same heatwave, opposite outcome. So whatever protects Banco Capiro lives inside this bay — and it's most likely several things at once, each turning a threat into an advantage.

Four suspects

MechanismWhat the model showsProtects
Turbidity shadeMeasured murk cuts the light half of heat stress — strongest during floodsdeep bank
Urchin grazingDiadema at ~100× density carry the grazing the missing fish can'twhole reef
River chemistryIf the Ulúa is limestone-fed, its alkalinity funds calcificationgrowth
Heat-tolerant algaeNeeds genetics to confirm; likely carries the shallow elkhornshallow coral

What we've ruled out

The obvious "it just got lucky" explanations don't survive the models — which is what makes the reef genuinely interesting:

Ruled outWhy
It's cooler here40 yrs of satellite SST: same regional temperature as the degrading reefs; it hit DHW 14 in 2023 and lived
Currents divert the riverParticle model: the plume reaches the reef — but flushes through in ~4 days, so the bay gets turbidity's shade without smothering
Deep upwelling cools itGLORYS: no cool subsurface pool, and the coast is downwelling-favorable (1/12° caveat noted)

Three physical escapes gone. What's left is local biology and optics — the four suspects above — which is exactly what the expedition goes to measure.

How the trip cracks it — five stations

01

Bleaching-vs-depth survey + water clarity

The most diagnostic test. If shallow corals bleach more than deep ones in heat, turbidity shading is confirmed. Log light at 3, 10, 20 m at Banco Capiro and a clear reference reef.

PAR logger / Secchidepth-tagged transectWIN: shallow bleaches > deep
02

Symbiont tissue samples

The one mechanism we can't compute. Tissue punches for Symbiodiniaceae typing — is heat-tolerant Durusdinium dominant? Coordinate with Andrew Baker's lab (U. Miami).

tissue punch + preservativeWIN: heat-tolerant symbiont
03

Urchin & herbivore-fish transects

Turns the grazing model into a number. Count Diadema per m² and herbivorous fish on fixed transects.

belt transect + quadratWIN: high urchin, low fish
04

Water chemistry — river mouth & on-reef

Decides whether the pollution helps or harms. Sample alkalinity, DIC, nutrients, aragonite saturation. Key number: the river's alkalinity : DIC ratio.

bottle samples, fixedWIN: high river alkalinity
05

Temperature loggers at depth

Satellites miss daily and tidal swings. Loggers at the bank crest and base catch high-frequency variability and any cool upwelling pulses.

HOBO loggers ×2+WIN: cool pulses / high variance

Confirm with the Coral Reef Alliance team on site

  • Dive traffic: dives/year on Banco Capiro vs Roatán or Utila? (The busy reefs are the sick ones.)
  • Fishing effort: is the shallow coral genuinely avoided, and is the 2018 marine-park boundary enforced?
  • Bleaching history: which years and at what depth was bleaching observed here?
  • Sediment plume: when/where does the Ulúa plume reach the bank — season, after storms?
