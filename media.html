Fast facts

~59% live coral in 2014, four times the Caribbean average.
−31% coral and +193% seaweed after the 2022 sea-urchin die-off.
DHW 14 heat stress in 2023, deep in the mass-death zone, survived.
2.5× murkier and 4.2× more nutrient-rich than nearby healthy reefs.
~800 colonies of critically endangered elkhorn coral.
2018 declared a protected marine park (~822 km²).

LINKEDIN / LONGER
Coral reefs are collapsing worldwide, so a reef that thrives through heat and pollution is worth understanding.

Banco Capiro, off northern Honduras, holds 60 to 70% living coral in warm, murky, sewage-fed water that is killing the famous clear reefs nearby. It took a mass-mortality-level heat dose in 2023 and lived.

We rebuilt its situation from physics and biology. Three easy explanations failed: it is not cooler, the river is not steered away, there is no cool upwelling. What is left is local biology, murky water that shades the coral, nutrients the coral can eat, sea urchins that mow the seaweed, and likely a heat-tolerant algae inside the coral.

A team is heading there to measure the clues. This reef may be a blueprint for keeping coral alive in a warming ocean.
